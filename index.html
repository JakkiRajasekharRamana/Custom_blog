The Marvel Cinematic Universe

The Marvel Cinematic Universe is a series of interconnected superhero films and television shows produced by Marvel Studios, based on characters from Marvel Comics. It all began with "Iron Man" in 2008, and has since grown to become one of the most successful and popular film franchises of all time.

The MCU is known for its complex and interconnected storyline, with each film and show contributing to a larger narrative that spans multiple characters and franchises

Overall, the Marvel Cinematic Universe is a vast and exciting world full of action, humor, and unforgettable characters, and it continues to grow with each new film and show that is released.

There are currently four phases in the Marvel Cinematic Universe (MCU).

Phase One includes the first six films of the MCU, starting with "Iron Man" (2008) and concluding with "The Avengers" (2012).

Phase Two includes six films, beginning with "Iron Man 3" (2013) and ending with "Ant Man (2015).

Phase Three includes eleven films, starting with ""Captain America: Civil War" (2016) and concluding with "Spider-Man: Far From Home" (2019).

Phase Four began with "Black Widow" (2021) and is set to include at least 10 projects, including movies and TV shows.
